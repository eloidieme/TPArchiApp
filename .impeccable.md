## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience: a professor reviewing a school assignment. The interface is used as a demonstration artifact for a client-side web exercise, so it needs to be immediately readable, credible, and easy to evaluate without explanation.

### Brand Personality
Modern, warm, and grounded. The interface should feel approachable and considered rather than experimental, flashy, or aggressively technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Support both light and dark themes. Avoid visuals that feel "AI-generated", especially cliché dark UIs with neon accents, generic startup styling, or overproduced effects. Favor editorial clarity, calm hierarchy, and human warmth over spectacle.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize legibility over decoration.
2. Make hierarchy obvious at a glance.
3. Keep interactions simple, calm, and credible.
4. Use warmth and polish without drifting into gimmicks.
5. Ensure both themes feel intentional, not like a superficial color swap.
